2009 Computer Science Contest Results

On March 21, 2009, thirty-four students competed in the department’s annual programming contest. The contestants had about three hours to solve ten problems posed by the computer science faculty. Michael Chest, a senior Computer Science major, won the contest, correctly solving nine of the problems, with a partial solution of the tenth. John Sidwell, a senior Computer Science major, also solved nine problems to place a close second; and Ian Wagner, a sophomore Computer Science major, placed third. Congratulations to these and the other students who participated.

Special thanks to Planet Technologies and The Worthwhile Company for sponsoring the contest.

Results are shown below in order of ranking. In the case of ties, partial solutions (Sample Solved) and penalty points were used to determine ranking.

Note that Jordan Jueckstock and Mark Ladygo, as past winners of the contest, participated and are included in the results summary, but were not eligible to win again.
