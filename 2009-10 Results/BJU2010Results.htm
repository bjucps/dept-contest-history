2010 BJU Programming Contest Results


On Saturday, March 20, 2010, 27 students participated in the Computer Science Programming Contest, a tradition since 1990. Contestants had about 3 hours to solve a series of problems with graduated difficulties.

First Place – Travis McGee, a sophomore computer science major, correctly solving 6 of the problems
Second Place – Mark Ladygo, a senior computer engineering major, solving 6 problems
Third Place – David Johnson, a senior computer science major

Mr. Dan Wooster, a Department of Computer Science faculty member, said, “One of the reasons for these programming contests is to help draw attention to the need for good software developers.” According to statistics issued by the U.S. Department of Labor, the growth of and need for IT jobs will strongly increase in the next few years.

Special thanks to Worthwhile, Inc., M-33 Integrated Solutions, and Answers in Genesis for sponsoring the contest.
